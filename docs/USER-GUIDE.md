## Creating Personas

### What is a Persona?

A persona is an authentic subset of yourself, emphasized for a specific context.

**It's not:**
- A lie or deception
- A mask hiding the real you
- Multiple personalities

**It is:**
- A genuine filter on your complete self
- Different emphasis based on context
- Honest representation of how you show up

### Built-in Persona Types

#### The Architect
- **Emphasis:** Systems thinking, strategy, long-term vision
- **Core Skills:** Strategic planning, organizational thinking, big-picture perspective
- **Shows up in:** CEO role, CTO role, strategy consulting, business development
- **Typical profile:** 10+ years experience, team leadership, business metrics

#### The Engineer
- **Emphasis:** Problem-solving, implementation, shipping products
- **Core Skills:** Coding, debugging, optimization, rapid prototyping
- **Shows up in:** Software engineer role, technical leadership, startup CTO
- **Typical profile:** Deep technical depth, project completion, concrete results

#### The Technician
- **Emphasis:** Precision, craftsmanship, quality, details
- **Core Skills:** Testing, documentation, process improvement, reliability
- **Shows up in:** QA engineer, operations, infrastructure, site reliability engineering
- **Typical profile:** Error reduction, systematic improvements, best practices

#### The Creator
- **Emphasis:** Innovation, originality, aesthetic sensibility, novel approaches
- **Core Skills:** Design thinking, creative problem-solving, communication, storytelling
- **Shows up in:** Product designer, UX researcher, technical writer, creative technologist
- **Typical profile:** Published work, design portfolio, thought leadership

#### The Connector
- **Emphasis:** Communication, relationships, collaboration, influence
- **Core Skills:** Networking, negotiation, team building, conflict resolution
- **Shows up in:** Sales, business development, community organizing, leadership
- **Typical profile:** Network reach, partnership building, team impact

#### The Analyst
- **Emphasis:** Data-driven thinking, research, investigation, rigor
- **Core Skills:** Research methodology, statistical thinking, pattern recognition
- **Shows up in:** Data scientist, researcher, business analyst, UX researcher
- **Typical profile:** Research publications, data projects, insights driven

### Create Custom Personas

You can also define your own personas:

1. **Give it a name** that feels authentic (e.g., "The Resilient Rebuilder")
2. **Write a brief description** (2-3 sentences about how this persona shows up)
3. **Assign a tone** (how does this persona communicate? Formal? Casual? Creative?)
4. **Define core values** (what matters most to this persona?)
5. **Set visibility** (who sees this persona? Everyone? Specific employers?)
6. **Add a motto** (optional Latin epigraph or personal motto)

**Example:**
```
Name: The Rebuilder
Description: Someone who has walked through fire and come out stronger. 
  Understands resilience, empathy, and the power of second chances.
Tone: Honest, vulnerable, grounded
Core Values: Authenticity, growth, paying it forward, human connection
Visibility: Everyone who asks
Motto: "From ash, growth"
```

4. **Apply** - Send with personalized cover letter

Rather than sending 2000 applications to generic roles, you'll send 10-20 applications to carefully evaluated matches.

## Inverted Interviews

### The Traditional Interview Problem

Traditional interviews:
- ‚ùå Employer controls the questions
- ‚ùå Candidate hopes to impress
- ‚ùå Information is asymmetric
- ‚ùå Red flags only emerge after you're hired
- ‚ùå Power dynamic favors the employer

### The Inverted Interview Solution

In‚Äìmidst‚Äìmy‚Äìlife **flips the dynamic**:

When an employer visits your profile to review you, they're automatically presented with **YOUR questions about THEM**.

### The Five Evaluation Categories

Employers answer questions across five dimensions:

#### 1. Cultural Fit (You evaluate them)

Your questions:
- What does a typical day look like? (honesty check)
- How are conflicts handled? (management style)
- What's your turnover rate? Why do people leave? (health indicator)
- How diverse is your team? What's your track record? (values alignment)
- What's the communication style? (async, sync, open, structured?)

**Their answers reveal:**
- Whether they're honest about challenges
- How much turnover they're hiding
- Whether diversity is tokenism or genuine
- Management quality
- Alignment with your working style

#### 2. Growth Potential (What will you learn?)

Your questions:
- What skills will I develop? (specific examples)
- Who are my mentors? How available are they?
- What's your track record with career progression? (promotions, laterals)
- Do you invest in training/conferences/development?
- How much autonomy do I have to shape my role?

**Their answers reveal:**
- Whether they invest in people
- If mentorship is real or theoretical
- Growth ceiling (how far can you go?)
- Learning culture

#### 3. Compensation & Benefits

Your questions:
- Full compensation transparency (base, bonus, equity structure)
- What's your equity timeline? (vesting, refresh grants?)
- Health insurance and benefits details
- Work-life balance expectations (hours, on-call, travel?)
- Remote work policy and culture

**Their answers reveal:**
- Whether they're transparent
- If comp is competitive or they're undercut
- Hidden expectations (crunch culture?)
- How much they value work-life balance

#### 4. Sustainability (Will they survive?)

Your questions:
- Business health (runway, profitability, growth trajectory)
- What's your capital strategy? (next funding, path to profitability?)
- How's your customer retention? (sign of product quality)
- How stable is your market?
- What are your biggest risks?

**Their answers reveal:**
- Job security likelihood
- Whether they're honest about challenges
- Whether you'll have stability to do good work

#### 5. Impact & Purpose (Does it align?)

Your questions:
- What impact are you trying to have?
- Who does your product serve? Who doesn't?
- How do you handle ethical trade-offs?
- What's your environmental/social impact?
- How do you measure success beyond revenue?

**Their answers reveal:**
- Whether impact is real or marketing
- Ethical alignment
- Whether this is a job or a mission

### How It Works

1. **You set up your profile** with your evaluation criteria
2. **Employer visits your profile** to view your background
3. **They see your evaluation form** with your 5-category questions
4. **They must answer** to see your full profile
5. **You review their answers** before scheduling interviews
6. **Both parties know where they stand** before meeting

### The Mutual Benefit

**For You:**
- Red flags emerge before you invest time
- You maintain power in the process
- You interview people who respect you
- You find culture fits, not just job fits

**For Employers:**
- They attract candidates who are serious and thoughtful
- They demonstrate confidence in their company
- They signal to candidates they have nothing to hide
- They pre-filter for candidates aligned with their values

## FAQ

### General Questions

**Q: Is this a lie? Am I being deceptive?**

A: No. You're being contextual. Everyone does this naturally:
- You speak differently to your boss than to your kids
- You emphasize different skills to different people
- You're authentic in all contexts, just with different emphasis

In‚Äìmidst‚Äìmy‚Äìlife makes this explicit and honest instead of implicit and hidden.

**Q: What if employers figure out I have multiple personas?**

A: You can tell them. Many will appreciate the honesty and self-awareness. And yes, your personas are linked‚Äîthey can see that "Engineer John" and "Architect John" are the same person if you want them to.

The question isn't "do you have personas?" Everyone does. The question is "are you honest about them?"

**Q: How much should I include? Isn't this overwhelming?**

A: Include everything that represents real capability. The system filters it. Your resume won't be 5 pages‚Äîit'll be 1-2 pages tailored to each role, because we only show relevant experience.

### Technical Questions

**Q: How secure is my data?**

A: 
- All data encrypted at rest (AES-256)
- HTTPS in transit (TLS 1.3)
- No data shared without your consent
- You can export or delete anytime
- See [docs/SECURITY.md](../docs/SECURITY.md) for details

**Q: Can I make my profile private?**

A: Yes. You can:
- Share only with specific employers
- Set up a private profile with password protection
- Create a public summary while keeping details private
- Control visibility of each experience

**Q: What if I change my mind? Can I update my profile?**

A: Yes, anytime:
- Edit any experience
- Adjust persona definitions
- Change visibility settings
- Update job search criteria
- All changes take effect immediately

**Q: Can I have multiple profiles?**

A: Yes. You might want:
- One for tech roles
- One for leadership roles
- One for creative work
- One for teaching/mentoring

Each profile is independent.

### Hunter Protocol Questions

**Q: How does the Hunter find jobs?**

A: The Hunter searches:
- LinkedIn Jobs
- Angel List
- RemoteOk
- Company career pages
- Custom job feeds
- (More sources coming)

It filters using your criteria and analyzes each match.

**Q: Can I modify the AI's analysis?**

A: Yes. You can:
- Add notes to assessments
- Override scores
- Save assessments for reference
- Share with mentors or advisors

**Q: What if I want to apply manually?**

A: The Hunter is optional. You can:
- Manually tailored resume from any persona
- Use the persona selection to regenerate your resume
- Copy/paste into any application

### Persona Questions

**Q: Can I have more than 5 personas?**

A: Yes. You can create as many personas as are authentic to you. We recommend 2-5, but some people have more.

**Q: How do I avoid personas feeling like lies?**

A: Personas ARE you, just with different emphasis. Test this:
- Is a persona something you've genuinely done?
- Have you succeeded in that context?
- Would people who know you recognize it as authentically you?

If yes to all three, it's a persona, not a lie.

**Q: How do I know which persona to use for a job?**

A: The system recommends based on job description analysis. But you can also:
- Ask mentors which persona resonates
- Try different personas and see which feels right
- Mix personas for complex roles
